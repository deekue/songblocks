Song Blocks

A no-tech, toddler-friendly controller for Sonos using NFC tags

Welcome to Song Blocks

I built Song Blocks so my toddler could play music on the living room Sonos by himself.

Wood blocks containing NFC tags magnetically attach to a drawer face, behind which sits a Raspberry Pi and an NFC controller. Each block's NFC tag is mapped to a song, which plays when the block attaches and stops when it is detached. Each played song is tweeted at @songblocks.

The primary design goal was to make the technology disappear completely: the final experience is organic, tactile and aural - and quite magical. The blocks align and pull themselves to the drawer face, snapping on with a satisfying clack!. Music fills the room until the block is prised away. @songblocks silently records a history my son's listening habits. It will be fun to graph it in a few months to see how his preferences have changed over time.

Technologies Used

  • Raspberry Pi
  • NFC controller board
  • NFC tags
  • Rare-earth magnets
  • A simple Python script (~100 lines) I wrote, hosted here
  • Open-source Python libs: SoCo to talk to the Sonos, nfcpy to talk to the NFC controller, TweetPony to talk to Twitter

Making the blocks

I used 3/8" thick walnut boards and cut them down to pieces that were about 1-1/2"x3-1/4". Two of these form each block.
Cavities were routed out for the NFC tag and magnets The halves were then glued and clamped Each side was cleaned up by sawing off a very thin slice on the band saw The edges were given a 1/8" radius round-over with a router The sides and edges were sanded with 120 grit (the seam between the halves were rendered barely visible) The numbers were imprinted with a ink stamp set, then burned in with a soldering iron I've opted to leave the wood natural - no oil, sealant, etc.

The drawer

Cavities were routed out from the inside of the drawer face to place the magnets The NFC controller board is stuck on to the inside of the drawer face with electrical tape The Raspberry Pi just sits in the drawer, with its power cord running out the back
